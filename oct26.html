images & methods

Jarom Vogel

I discovered Jarom Vogel this week. He's basically doing exactly what I want to do with interactive illustration!! I might have to email him and ask him some questions, because I feel like his skills might be a little beyond my reach at the moment. But his parallax stuff, especially for mobile application, is amazing. And I love his illustrations beyond that too.

I went to a motion graphics workshop as part of St. Louis Design Week and also fiddled around a bit in AfterEffects this week. Motion remains a big interest of mine, and I'd like to explore AfterEffects further to see what I can do with it and how it can blend with my illustration work. I think designing for motion would be interesting.

Finally, Illustrator's Sketchbook visited Riso Hell this week to print our risograph illustrations. The project was definitely a challenge! I had to think in a completely different way from my usual illustration mentality. My prints only turned out okay, but it was a great learning experience and really fun to see what my classmates pulled off.

readings

Tyrus Wong

Read an interesting piece in The New York Times about an artist named Tyrus Wong, who did absolutely incredible art direction for Disney on Bambi and was not properly credited for his work for decades due to racism. His paintings are beautiful; the way he simplifies form and uses color is totally unique. His life story is sad. A reminder that a lot of people can be screwed over in the making of amazing art.
