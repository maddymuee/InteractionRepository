readings

In my composition class this week, we read two essays on artistic style (an excerpt from Against Interpretation by Susan Sontag and "Form vs. Content, The Eternal Conversation" by John Keene)—specifically literary style, but the discussion can definitely be applied to art.

The first reading argued that style and content should not be separated, and that in fact style actually informs content (definitely the opposite of conventional design thought). She also declared that art, if it was real art, could not be activist/political regardless of the author’s intentions. This was pretty weird to me given that I’m also in a class called Art & Activism.

The second reading—a much different take—questioned whether or not style is political and whether art can ever not be political. It got me thinking about whether or not style can be ethical, and if the ethics of style apply to design, where we’re often encouraged to be as invisible as possible.
