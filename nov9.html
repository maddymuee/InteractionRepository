Paul Rand

Took a bit of a dive back into Paul Rand's work while researching for a freelance project I'm doing under David Rygiol's direction. I so admire the simplicity here!!! I feel like I always overwork my stuff. His work is instantly memorable just because of the colors and shapes. Sigh...

This week was kind of another breakthrough in terms of my own work. I started using my own drawings(!) in Interaction, something I honestly should have been doing and have wanted to do from the start (why it took me this long is a mystery). Learned some more JavaScript. In Sketchbook, found a way to make drawing a Rube Goldberg machine (blech) be genuinely fun. Seems like the move now is just to give in to my whims as much as possible?

readings

Have been reading some fiction about TV including "The Veldt" by Ray Bradbury, a speculative fiction sci-fi/horror piece about a VR room in a smart home. It has been fascinating to read about people's anxieties about a technology that is now becoming antiquated. I feel pretty anxious about certain emerging technologies right now—maybe kids in the future will laugh at me, too.

Started re-reading Through the Looking-Glass. I actually forgot how spooky it is and now I'm having second thoughts about using it rather than Wonderland. We'll see.
